Common Latched circuit Application

1.Turn on the switch, the light will be on;
2.Turn of the switch the light will be off
X0 is on pushbutton
X1 is off pushbutton


When x0 (normally open) is pressed y0 (light) will turn on and when x1(normally closed)is pressed y0 will turn off. Since it is pushbutton, latching is required in order for light to remain on untill and unless x1 is pressed.

Interlock control circuit Application

The Entry/Exit of the parking lot is a single lanne passage. By controlling the indicator, the program ensures that only one car can pass through the Entry/Exit so as to prevent car accident between entering and leaving cars

X0 : Car entering sensor.When a car passes through the sensor,X0=on
X1 :Car leaving sensor.When a car passes through the sensor,X1=on
y0:Entering Car indicator( on means go and off means stop)
y0:leaving Car indicator( on means go and off means stop)

When x0 sensor turns on that means car has entered and y0 will turn on. but y1 should be off when y0 is on in order to prevent accident . Therefore y0 places normally closed in series with x1 sensor so that y0(leaving indicator) should not be turned on

Practical Application

1.A wood saw, a fan and a lubrication pump, all go ON when the start button is turned On. A stop button when pressed stops the saw only. The fan has to run an additional 5 sec to blow the chip away and the lubrication pump has to run for 8 sec after the shutdown of the wood saw. Draw the Plc ladder diagram

consider X0 as start pushbutton.
When start button is pressed, a wood saw,fan and lubricating pump should remain on therefor latching is required
When stop button is pressed the saw turns off immidiately while the fan and lubricating pump turns off after 5 and 8 sec respectively
Therefore 2 timer is triggered immidiately when the stop button is pressed.
Since fan and lubricating pump should stop,timers are placed normally closed in series in that respective rung.

Practical Application: To calculate speed and length of the bag

x0 is photoelectric Sensor 1
x1 is photoelectric Sensor 2

Requireent: 2 Sensors placed at some known distance, consider 0.5meters
From distance formula speed=distance/time
First we have to calculate speed at which bag is moving or the speed of the conveyor
If the speed is not known we can calculate it using 2 sensors placed at 0.5 distance
speed=0.5/time taken by the bag to travel from first sensor to the second sensor.

Timer T250 is a retentive timer. It turns on as soon as sensor X0 detects the bag and when second sensor detects the bag, the time is stored in some data register
when 0.5m distance is divided by this time we get speed of the conveyor
